Welcome to Manchester's Didsbury Park, and to the 'Friends of Didsbury Park' website where you will find information on the park, it's very active 'Volunteers', how to become a member and what's going on. We hope this website will encourage visitors to the park and, by reading Brian's Blog, keep up to date on what's happening, In addition to our regular work in the park this year we have been working especially hard on the Poppy Path. Well done to everyone. Click here to follow progress on the path on Twitter or on the twitter logo for tweets on the park Every Monday morning (including Bank Holidays) the volunteers continue to work in the park. Newcomers are always made welcome, just fill in the Contact Form to let us know you are interested. We are looking to expand the membership base of the Friends of Didsbury Park in 2019! Come and join us and have a say in the development of the park. Head over to membership to find out more.

The National 'Britain in Bloom' competition is a nationwide community gardening campaign helping to transform cities, towns & villages. The scheme, which is the largest of its kind in Europe, is run by the RHS (Royal Horticultural Society). For more information click here. Didsbury Park is pleased to be part of 'Didsbury in Bloom' so the Volunteers are working hard on bringing colour into the park. Last year (2017) the park was awarded Gold, see certificate on the right and this year (2018) 'Outstanding'. Our thanks go to everyone who has helped achieve this. Regular meetings are held to monitor progress and encourage participants. However more volunteers are needed, particularly on village clean up days. If you want to help please contact us or 'Friends of Fletcher Moss and Parsonage Gardens' who lead the 'Didsbury in Bloom' entry.
